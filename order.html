Order

Order is the 2nd operator in the BODMAS rule

Any orders are done after anything in brackets if there are any

Orders can refer to either powers, indicies, roots or exponents (For example 22 = 4, 33 = 27)

Remember the order in which you work through a calculation is according to ORDER OF IMPORTANCE

Below are some examples of BODMAS questions using order to give you an idea:

  • 22 + 8 = 4 + 8 = 12
  • 3 x 52 = 3 x 25 = 75
  • 43 ÷ 4 = 64 ÷ 10 = 16

Below are some examples of BODMAS questions using order and where you might go wrong:

    2. Answer = 23

    Potential mistake: You may end up calculating 7 + 4 as you may know to work from left to right for equations but, in BODMAS you work based on the order of importance, therefore you must do calculate the order, then you multify and finally add.
